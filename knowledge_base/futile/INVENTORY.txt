DOCUMENT: GLOBAL AFTER-SALES INVENTORY POLICY (v2026.1)
SECTION 1: SKU CLASSIFICATION METHODOLOGY
All automotive spare parts must be categorized using a dual-axis ABC/XYZ matrix.
- Category A: High-value items accounting for 80% of total consumption value. Requires bi-weekly cycle counts.
- Category B: Medium-value items (15% of value). Requires quarterly auditing.
- Category C: Low-value/High-volume items (5% of value). Managed via automated reorder points.

SECTION 2: STOCKING PROTOCOLS FOR EV COMPONENTS
Due to chemical degradation risks, Lithium-Ion battery modules (Series-E) must be stored in climate-controlled zones maintained at 18°C to 22°C. 
Safety Stock Level (SS) = (Maximum Lead Time - Average Lead Time) × Average Daily Demand.
If the SS drops below 15%, the "Critical Shortage" flag must be triggered in the ERP.

SECTION 3: LEAD TIME ASSUMPTIONS
- Domestic Tier-1 Suppliers: 5-7 business days.
- International OEM Parts: 21-30 business days.
- Localized Aftermarket Plastic Trim: 3 business days.
